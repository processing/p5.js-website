A double pendulum is a simple system that exhibits chaotic motion. This simulation utilizes **Verlet Integration** to track the system’s state by comparing current and previous positions, a method far more stable for multi-body systems than standard Euler integration. To enforce the "rigid" rods, we employ a **Gauss-Seidel** constraint solver that runs 15 iterations per frame, iteratively "relaxing" the bobs into their correct positions. Because numerical simulations naturally "leak" energy through discretization and constraint damping, we monitor the system’s **Hamiltonian** (total energy) and apply a corrective scaling factor. By injecting a small amount of stochastic noise into this correction, we simulate a "driven" system—like a pendulum on a rickety train—ensuring the motion remains perpetually chaotic and never loses its kinetic "kick."
