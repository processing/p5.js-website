description: >
  <p>Draws an image to the canvas.</p>

  <p>The first parameter, <code>img</code>, is the source image to be drawn.
  <code>img</code> can be

  any of the following objects:</p>

  <ul>

  <li><a href="/reference/p5/p5.Image">p5.Image</a></li>

  <li><a href="/reference/p5/p5.Element">p5.Element</a></li>

  <li><a href="/reference/p5/p5.Texture">p5.Texture</a></li>

  <li><a href="/reference/p5/p5.Framebuffer">p5.Framebuffer</a></li>

  <li><a
  href="/reference/p5/p5.FramebufferTexture">p5.FramebufferTexture</a></li>

  </ul>

  <p>The second and third parameters, <code>dx</code> and <code>dy</code>, set
  the coordinates of the

  destination image's top left corner. See

  <a href="/reference/p5/imageMode/">imageMode()</a> for other ways to position
  images.</p>

  <p>Here's a diagram that explains how optional parameters work in
  <code>image()</code>:</p>

  <p><img src="/assets/drawImage.png"/></p>

  <p>The fourth and fifth parameters, <code>dw</code> and <code>dh</code>, are
  optional. They set the

  the width and height to draw the destination image. By default,
  <code>image()</code>

  draws the full source image at its original size.</p>

  <p>The sixth and seventh parameters, <code>sx</code> and <code>sy</code>, are
  also optional.

  These coordinates define the top left corner of a subsection to draw from

  the source image.</p>

  <p>The eighth and ninth parameters, <code>sw</code> and <code>sh</code>, are
  also optional.

  They define the width and height of a subsection to draw from the source

  image. By default, <code>image()</code> draws the full subsection that begins
  at

  <code>(sx, sy)</code> and extends to the edges of the source image.</p>

  <p>The ninth parameter, <code>fit</code>, is also optional. It enables a
  subsection of

  the source image to be drawn without affecting its aspect ratio. If

  <code>CONTAIN</code> is passed, the full subsection will appear within the
  destination

  rectangle. If <code>COVER</code> is passed, the subsection will completely
  cover the

  destination rectangle. This may have the effect of zooming into the

  subsection.</p>

  <p>The tenth and eleventh paremeters, <code>xAlign</code> and
  <code>yAlign</code>, are also

  optional. They determine how to align the fitted subsection.
  <code>xAlign</code> can

  be set to either <code>LEFT</code>, <code>RIGHT</code>, or
  <code>CENTER</code>. <code>yAlign</code> can be set to

  either <code>TOP</code>, <code>BOTTOM</code>, or <code>CENTER</code>. By
  default, both <code>xAlign</code> and <code>yAlign</code>

  are set to <code>CENTER</code>.</p>
